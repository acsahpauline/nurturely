early warning signs.

the subtle shifts most parents miss.

1

food avoidance

Refusing foods they once loved, skipping meals, or suddenly "watching what they eat" without any medical reason.

2

excessive mirror time

Spending unusually long periods in front of mirrors, pinching skin, or making negative comments about their body.

3

hoarding beauty products

Secretly collecting makeup, skincare, or appearance-altering products beyond age-appropriate curiosity.

4

social withdrawal

Avoiding social situations, especially those involving food, swimwear, or physical activity with peers.

5

toxic content consumption

Obsessively following influencers or accounts centered around extreme body standards, diets, or looksmaxxing.

6

negative self talk

Frequently saying "I'm fat", "I'm ugly", or comparing themselves to unrealistic standards — even as a joke.

recognise any of these in your child?

about nurturely.

built with intention. designed with care.

we built nurturely because the most dangerous moment in a child's life often looks like nothing at all.

our mission

To help parents become the first line of defense against body image issues in children aged 10-14, by giving them the awareness, tools, and guidance to act early and act right.

why it matters

Body image issues rarely announce themselves. They hide in skipped meals, long mirror sessions, and jokes about being "too fat." By the time most parents notice, the pattern is already deep.

our approach

We don't surveil. We don't diagnose. We guide parents to show up with sensitivity, not suspicion, so their child feels safe enough to open up when it matters most.

our belief

The best intervention is not a therapist or a warning label. It is a parent who knows how to listen, when to speak, and how to make their child feel seen.
